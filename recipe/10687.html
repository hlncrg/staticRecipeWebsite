Mrs. Sigg's Snickerdoodles

Original Ingredients
  • 1/2 cup butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup shortening
  • 1 1/2 cups white sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 2 3/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons cream of tartar
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons white sugar
  • 2 teaspoons ground cinnamon

Common Modifications

decrease shortening

Used a full cup of Earth Balance non-hydrogenated 'margarine,' instead of doing half shortening.

I used all unsalted butter instead of shortening and also added a tablespoon of cinnamin mixed in the dough.

decrease cream

I reduced the cream of tartar down to 1 and 1/2 teaspoons per another reviewers suggestion.

Substitute 1 tsp cream of tartar ALWAYS with 1 teaspoon fresh lemon juice.

reviews are mixed for vanilla

I used some vanilla that my aunt brought back from the Dominican Republic, which gave the cookies amazing flavor.

I added an extra teaspoon of vanilla to mine.

Used almond extract instead of vanilla.

increase cinnamon sugar

sprinkled with cinnamon sugar mix after baking too while still hot so it absorbed.

Third, I doubled the cinnamon sugar mixture, because I ran out.

decrease vinegar

I also had to substitute vinegar for the cream of tartar (2 teaspoons vinegar= 1 teaspoon tartar).

decrease baking soda

if you like super fluffy cookies use 3 teaspoons of baking powder INSTEAD of the baking soda.
